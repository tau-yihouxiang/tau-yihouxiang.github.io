X-Ray: A Sequential 3D Representation for Generation

Abstract

We introduce a novel lightweight 3D representation, called X-Ray, for image to 3D generation, inspired by the principles of X-Ray shooting process. At its core, our approach leverages ray casting from the camera's center to record geometric and textural attributes encountered on all intersected surfaces. This methodology enables the compression of entire objects or scenes into a multi-layered surface representation. Subsequently, these surface attributes are organized into grid voxels, ensuring that only the essential surface information constitutes the 3D representation. The efficiency and versatility of the proposed X-Ray representation are demonstrated through its application in 3D object synthesis, including Text-to-3D and Image-to-3D transformations. Our proposed representation demonstrates significant improvements in both the fidelity and efficiency of 3D synthesis, offering promising avenues for future research and application.

Samples of X-Ray

Fig. 2. Samples of our proposed X-Ray Representation. The first row displays the raw 3D objects to be converted. The second row illustrates the images rendered from random camera perspectives. Subsequent rows reveal the hit H, depth D, normal N, and color C Maps of X-Ray from the 1st to the 8th layer. It is important to note that the number of layers in an arbitrary X-Ray is not fixed, indicating various complexity of 3D objects.

Synthesis Pipeline of X-Ray

Fig. 3. Overview of the 3D Generator for our proposed X-Ray. In the first stage, the diffusion model synthesizes a low-resolution X-Ray from random noise with text or image as condition. During the second stage, we adopt a Spatial-Temporal Upsampler to generate high-quality X-Ray. Finally, X-Ray can be directly converted to 3D Mesh via the decoding process.
